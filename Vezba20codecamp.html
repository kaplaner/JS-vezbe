Neil Armstrong

The first man who walk on the Moon

Neil Alden Armstrong (August 5, 1930 â€“ August 25, 2012) was an American astronaut, engineer, and the first person to walk on the Moon. He was also an aerospace engineer, naval aviator, test pilot, and university professor. Before becoming an astronaut, Armstrong was an officer in the U.S. Navy and served in the Korean War. After the war, he earned his bachelor's degree at Purdue University and served as a test pilot at the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA) High-Speed Flight Station, where he logged over 900 flights. He later completed graduate studies at the University of Southern California.

Here is a timeline of Armstrong's life:


  • 1930.
    Armstrong was born on August 5, near Wapakoneta, Ohio.
  • 1947.
    At age 17, Armstrong began studying aeronautical engineering at Purdue University.
  • 1949.
    Navy requiring him to report to Naval Air Station Pensacola for flight training at age 18.
  • 1950.
    Armstrong was informed by letter that he was a fully qualified Naval Aviator.
  • 1951.
    Armstrong first saw action in the Korean War on August 29, as an escort for a photo reconnaissance plane over Songjin.
  • 1956.
    He met Janet Elizabeth Shearon. They were married on January 28, at the Congregational Church in Wilmette, Illinois.
  • 1957.
    Armstrong's first flight in a rocket plane was on August 15, in the Bell X-1B, to an altitude of 11.4 miles (18.3 km).
  • 1958.
    Armstrong had been selected for the U.S. Air Force's Man In Space Soonest program.
  • 1962.
    He was selected by the U.S. Air Force as one of seven pilot-engineers who would fly the space plane when it got off the design board.
  • 1966.
    The mission Gemini 8 was launched on March 16.
  • 1969.
    The landing on the surface of the Moon occurred several seconds after 20:17:40 UTC on July 20. He then turned and set his left boot on the lunar surface at 2:56 UTC July 21.
  • 1970.
    Armstrong completed his Master of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Southern California.
  • 1971.
    He accepted a teaching position in the Department of Aerospace Engineering at the University of Cincinnati.
  • 1979.
    After teaching for eight years, he resigned without explaining his reason for leaving.
  • 1992.
    He had met his second wife, Carol Held Knight, at a golf tournament, where they were seated together at the breakfast table.
  • 1994.
    They were married on June 12, in Ohio, and then had a second ceremony, at San Ysidro Ranch, in California.
  • 2012.
    Armstrong underwent bypass surgery on August 7.
  • 2012.
    He died on August 25, in Cincinnati, Ohio.

"Neil will always be remembered for taking humankind's first small step on a world beyond our own, but it was the courage, grace, and humility he displayed throughout this life that lifted him above the stars."

Eulogy for Neil by Charles Bolden, Administrator of NASA
